I am a graduate student at Stanford University interested in the neural mechanisms by which humans learn from reinforcement

I work with Dr. Samuel McClure and Dr. Anthony Wagner. I use behavioral, computational, and imaging methods to study how people represent and learn about relationships in the world. I am particularly interested in how different states of the environment are represented and the role of context in shaping these representations.

 

Formerly, I worked with Dr. Alison Adcock and Dr. Vishnu Murty at Duke University on systems-level approaches to cognitive neuroscience. This work focuses on the role of the mesolimbic dopamine system and its afferents in motivation and novelty processing.

 

My current research interests also include reinforcement learning models, intertemporal choice, Bayesian approaches to data fitting, and computational cognitive modeling.
